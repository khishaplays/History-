Professor Wangari Maathai

The first African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize.

About Wangari

Wangari Maathai was a Kenyan environmentalist, political activist, and founder of the Green Belt Movement. She dedicated her life to environmental conservation, women's empowerment, and sustainable development.

Some Major Achievements

  • Founded the Green Belt Movement in 1977.
  • Planted trees across Kenya hence promoting aforestation.
  • Won the Nobel Peace Prize in 2004.
  • Advocated for democracy and human rights.

Famous Quotes

"It's the little things citizens do. That's what will make the difference."

Why we remember her

Wangari Maathai inspired people worldwide to protect the environment and stand up for social justice. Her work continues to influence generations today.
